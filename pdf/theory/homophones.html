Homophones & Homonyms

Different words that sound alike or are spelled alike.

What Are Homophones?

Homophones are words that sound the same but have different meanings and spellings.

  • to, too, two – different meanings but pronounced the same.
  • sea, see – words that sound alike but mean different things.

What Are Homonyms?

Homonyms are words that have the same spelling or pronunciation but different meanings. Sometimes homonyms are also homophones.

  • Bat: a flying mammal / a piece of sports equipment.
  • Bank: a financial institution / the side of a river.

Examples of Homophones

Word 1Word 2Meaning
WriteRightTo form letters / Correct
FlowerFlourA plant / Baking ingredient

HearHereTo listen / In this place

Why Are They Important?

Homophones and homonyms are important in English because they can cause confusion in writing and speaking. Understanding their differences improves clarity and communication.

Tips to Remember

  • Learn common homophones as pairs or groups.
  • Use context to understand the correct meaning.
  • Practice spelling and usage regularly.
