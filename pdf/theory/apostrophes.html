Apostrophes: Contractions & Possessives

Master it’s vs. its and you're vs. your to avoid confusion.

What Are Apostrophes?

Apostrophes are punctuation marks used to indicate contractions (shortened forms of words) and possessives (showing ownership).

Contractions

Contractions combine two words by omitting some letters and replacing them with an apostrophe.

  • It + is = It’s (It’s raining.)
  • You + are = You’re (You’re welcome.)
  • Do + not = Don’t (Don’t stop.)

Important: Contractions always use apostrophes to show missing letters.

Possessives

Possessive apostrophes show ownership or relationships.

  • The cat’s toy (The toy belongs to the cat.)
  • John’s book (The book belongs to John.)
  • The children’s playground (Belongs to multiple children.)

Tip: Unlike contractions, possessives show possession, not missing letters.

Common Confusions

It’s means "it is" or "it has" (contraction). Its shows possession (like "belonging to it").
You’re means "you are". Your shows possession (like "belonging to you").

Example: It’s a nice day. (It is) The dog lost its collar. (Possession) You’re my friend. (You are) Your book is on the table. (Possession)

Summary

Mastering apostrophes helps avoid common mistakes in English writing and improves clarity in communication.
