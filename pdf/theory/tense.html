English Tenses

Understand past, present, and future tenses with examples.

What Are Tenses?

Tenses tell us when an action happens â€” in the past, present, or future. They are an essential part of English grammar that help give context to sentences.

The Three Main Tenses

  • Past Tense: Actions that already happened.
  • Present Tense: Actions happening now or general truths.
  • Future Tense: Actions that will happen.

Simple Tenses Explained

Tense Usage Example
Simple Past Completed actions in the past. She visited Paris last year.
Simple Present Habitual actions or facts. He works every day.
Simple Future Actions that will happen. They will travel tomorrow.

Continuous (Progressive) Tenses

These tenses describe actions that are ongoing or in progress at a specific time.

Tense Usage Example
Past Continuous Actions happening at a specific time in the past. She was reading when I called.
Present Continuous Actions happening right now. He is eating lunch now.
Future Continuous Actions that will be ongoing in the future. We will be working at 10 a.m. tomorrow.

Perfect Tenses

Perfect tenses show actions that have been completed relative to a certain time.

Tense Usage Example
Past Perfect Action completed before another past action. She had finished before he arrived.
Present Perfect Actions completed at an unspecified time before now. They have traveled to many countries.
Future Perfect Actions that will be completed before a future time. By next year, I will have graduated.

Examples in Sentences

Simple Present: She writes letters every day.

Present Continuous: She is writing a letter now.

Simple Past: She wrote a letter yesterday.

Past Perfect: She had written the letter before dinner.

Simple Future: She will write a letter tomorrow.

Tips for Learning Tenses

  • Practice regularly with examples in speaking and writing.
  • Learn the common irregular verbs in past forms.
  • Use timelines to visualize when actions happen.
  • Watch videos or listen to native speakers to understand usage.
