Science Experiments

Learn fun science experiments you can try at home or in the classroom.

Introduction

Science experiments are a hands-on way to explore how the world works. They help us understand scientific concepts like chemistry, physics, and biology through simple activities. You don’t need a laboratory — many experiments can be done with items found at home.

Why Do Science Experiments?

  • They make learning fun and interactive.
  • They encourage curiosity and creativity.
  • They teach problem-solving and observation skills.
  • They help explain scientific principles in real life.

Easy Experiments to Try

1. Volcano Eruption

Combine baking soda and vinegar to create a fizzy, bubbling reaction that looks like lava.

Tip: Add red food coloring to make it look more realistic.

2. Rainbow in a Glass

Use sugar, water, and food coloring to create layers of colorful liquid, demonstrating density differences.

3. Invisible Ink

Write with lemon juice on paper. When heated, the writing appears as a brownish message.

Science Vocabulary

WordMeaning
ExperimentA test to discover or prove something.
HypothesisAn idea or prediction to be tested.
ReactionWhen substances change after being mixed.
ObservationLooking carefully at what happens.

Learning English with Science

Science experiments are a great way to practice English. Reading instructions, describing what you see, and learning new terms like "acid", "solution", or "reaction" improve your vocabulary and comprehension.
