Regular & Irregular Plurals

Understand plural formation and exceptions in English.

Introduction

In English, plurals are used to show that there is more than one of something. Most nouns form their plurals by adding -s or -es, but there are many irregular forms that follow different patterns.

Regular Plurals

Regular plurals are easy to form. Simply add -s to most nouns, or -es to words ending in -ch, -sh, -s, -x, or -z.

Examples: cat → cats, bus → buses, box → boxes.

Irregular Plurals

Some English words change form completely when pluralized, or stay the same. These are called irregular plurals, and they must be memorized.

Examples: child → children, man → men, woman → women, mouse → mice, sheep → sheep.

Common Plural Rules

RuleExample
Most nouns add -sdog → dogs
Nouns ending in s, x, z, ch, sh add -esbox → boxes
Nouns ending in consonant + y → change y to i + esbaby → babies
Irregular formsfoot → feet
Some nouns don't changefish → fish

Why Learn Plural Rules?

Mastering plural forms helps with speaking, writing, and reading English correctly. While regular plurals are simple, irregular ones are common and need practice.
