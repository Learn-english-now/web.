Silent Letters in English

Understand words with silent letters like "knight" and "psychology".

What Are Silent Letters?

Silent letters are letters in words that are written but not pronounced. They can make English spelling tricky, especially for learners, because the pronunciation doesnâ€™t always match the spelling.

Common Silent Letters and Examples

  • K is silent before n: knight, know, knee.
  • W is silent before r: write, wrong, wrist.
  • B is silent after m: comb, lamb, bomb.
  • GH is often silent: though, light, daughter.
  • P is silent in some words: psychology, pneumonia, receipt.

Tip: Knowing common silent letter patterns can help improve your spelling and pronunciation.

Why Do Silent Letters Exist?

Silent letters often remain from historical spellings or borrowed words from other languages like French or Latin. Over time, pronunciations changed, but spellings stayed the same.

Challenges for Learners

Silent letters can confuse learners, leading to misspellings and mispronunciations. Practice and exposure to English in context can help internalize these exceptions.

Summary

Understanding silent letters improves both your reading and writing skills. Pay attention to common patterns and practice often.
